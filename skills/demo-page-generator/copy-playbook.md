# Copy Playbook

The page is the copy. Visuals get attention; words convert.

---

## Headline Formulas (pick one)

### F1 — Outcome + Audience
> "[Outcome verb] [audience] [achieve / get / become]."
- "Where curiosity becomes confidence." (school)
- "Care that listens." (clinic)

### F2 — Provocative Reframe
> "[Common belief]. [Counter-statement]."
- "Toppers aren't born. They're trained." (coaching)
- "Slow food. Loud flavor." (restaurant)

### F3 — Two-Word Verdict
> "[Adjective] [noun]." OR a two-line couplet
- "Quiet expertise. Loud results." (law firm)
- "Breath first. Everything follows." (yoga)

### F4 — One-Word Authority
> A single bold word, with a sub-line carrying the explanation
- Headline: "Crafted." Sub: "Every dish, sourced within 100 km."

### Avoid
- ❌ "Welcome to [Brand Name]"
- ❌ "Your one-stop solution for..."
- ❌ "Best [service] in [city]"
- ❌ Anything starting with "We are..."

---

## Sub-headline Structure

One sentence. Maximum 18 words. Three components:

> **[Who it's for]** + **[What they get]** + **[How it's different]**

Example (clinic):
> "For families in Bandra who want unhurried consultations, comprehensive diagnostics, and a doctor who remembers your name."

Example (coaching):
> "For Class 11–12 students serious about JEE — small batches, IIT-mentor doubt sessions, and a study plan built around your weekest chapter."

## Service / Feature Copy

Each service tile has three parts:

1. **Title** (2–3 words, evocative): "Whole-Body Diagnostics" not "Health Check-Up"
2. **One-line description** (10–14 words): What you'll feel/get, not what we'll do
3. **Optional micro-detail** (a number or a name): "12 specialists on staff" / "30-minute results"

### Title transformations

| Generic | Evocative |
|---|---|
| "Health Check-Up" | "Whole-Body Diagnostics" |
| "Online Classes" | "Live, Recorded, Repeatable" |
| "Personal Training" | "Coached. Programmed. Tracked." |
| "Weight Loss Plan" | "Transformation, Engineered" |
| "Bridal Makeup" | "Wedding-Day Ritual" |
| "Tax Consulting" | "Returns Without Regret" |
| "Property Management" | "Concierge for Your Home" |

## Stats Bar

Every stat is **a number + a specific noun**. Verify the noun adds context.

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| "500+" | "500+ patients in care" |
| "98% satisfaction" | "98% would recommend us" |
| "12 years" | "12 years serving Pune" |
| "24/7" | "Doctor on call, 24/7" |

## Testimonial Anatomy

Every testimonial must contain **at least 3** of:
- ✅ A name (full first + last initial — "Anjali M.")
- ✅ A specific role / context ("mother of two, Andheri West")
- ✅ A named service or person at the business
- ✅ A measurable result OR concrete moment
- ✅ A small flaw or hesitation overcome (makes it real)

### Templates

**Clinic**:
> "I'd been to four doctors before Dr. Rao. She caught my pre-diabetes in one visit and built a plan I could actually follow. Six months in, my A1C is normal."
> — Sneha K., Pune

**School**:
> "My son was shy. He'd never raised his hand. Three months in, he's leading the science club. Ms. Pillai saw something in him we hadn't."
> — Anjali M., parent, Class 6

**Coaching**:
> "Got AIR 412 in JEE Advanced. The 1:1 doubt sessions with Rohit Sir were the difference — he made calculus click in two weeks."
> — Aryan G., IIT Bombay '26

**Gym**:
> "Lost 14 kg in 5 months without crash dieting. Coach Vinay built a plan around my desk job and back pain. I deadlift 100kg now."
> — Priya R., 34, marketing manager

### Avoid
- ❌ "Great service! Highly recommend!" — vague
- ❌ "John D. — 5 stars" — no story
- ❌ "Best in the business" — superlative without proof
- ❌ Generic stock photos as avatars (use a colored circle with the initial instead)

## CTA Buttons

### Primary CTA verbs (pick one per section)
- "Book a Consultation" (clinic)
- "Reserve a Tour" (school, society, restaurant)
- "Claim Your Free Trial" (gym, SaaS, coaching)
- "Schedule a Visit" (clinic, society)
- "Start Admission" (school)
- "Talk to a Specialist" (clinic, law firm)
- "Download Brochure" (school, society — secondary)
- "View Menu" / "View Schedule" / "Browse Services" (secondary)

### Secondary CTA pattern
Always offer a low-commitment alternative next to the primary.
- Primary: "Book Your Visit" + Secondary: "View Pricing →"
- Primary: "Start Admission" + Secondary: "Download Brochure →"

### Avoid
- ❌ "Click Here"
- ❌ "Submit"
- ❌ "Learn More" (only OK as a link, never as a primary CTA)
- ❌ "Contact Us" (replace with "Book a Call")

---

## Section Headings

Use one of these formats:

1. **Two-word category + period**: "The Process." / "The Method." / "The Difference."
2. **Question that frames an answer**: "Why families pick us." / "What changes for you."
3. **Bold statement**: "We don't do crash diets." / "Every case, our priority."

### Avoid
- ❌ "Our Services"
- ❌ "About Us"
- ❌ "What We Offer"
- ❌ "Why Choose Us" (replace with the value, not the question)

---

## Footer Tagline

A one-sentence manifesto, not a slogan.

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| "Your trusted health partner" | "Listening longer, since 2012." |
| "Quality education for all" | "We teach kids to think, not memorize." |
| "Best gym in town" | "No mirrors for Instagram. Just iron." |

## Voice Calibration by Personality

| Personality | Voice |
|---|---|
| **Editorial Dark / Light** | Considered. Long sentences allowed. Em-dashes welcome. |
| **Clinical Sharp** | Crisp. Short. Reassuring. Numbers over adjectives. |
| **Brutalist Volt** | Punchy. Fragments. Imperative mood. "Show up. Lift. Repeat." |
| **Warm Sanctuary** | Conversational. Inclusive ("we", "us", "together"). |
| **Sage Wellness** | Spacious. Pause-driven. Sentences that breathe. |
| **Neon Studio** | Confident. Tech-fluent. Slight wink. |
| **Soft Pediatric** | Gentle. Reassuring. Parent-to-parent. |

---

## Final Test

Read the page out loud. If any line:
- Sounds like a press release → rewrite
- Could appear on any other website → rewrite
- Has the word "solutions", "leverage", "synergy", "world-class", "cutting-edge" → rewrite

Specificity is the enemy of generic.
